The Holocaust was the systematic, state-sponsored persecution and murder of six million Jews by the Nazi regime and its collaborators. Adolf Hitler and the Nazi leadership established policies that targeted Jews and other groups considered undesirable. The persecution included legal discrimination, violence, and mass murder.

The Nazi regime organized a system of ghettos, forced labor, and extermination camps across German-controlled territories. Millions of victims were killed through shootings, starvation, forced labor, and gas chambers.

The Holocaust is often described as a crime against humanity and a warning about the dangers of racism, antisemitism, and unchecked state power. Museums and educational institutions emphasize remembrance, responsibility, and the protection of human rights.
